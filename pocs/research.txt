Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar is a former Indian international cricketer who played for the Indian national team. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket and is often referred to as "The Little Master" or "God of Cricket."

---

### 1. Key Concepts

*   **Legendary Batsman:** Tendulkar is universally acknowledged as one of the most prolific and technically sound batsmen cricket has ever seen, dominating bowlers across generations.
*   **Record Breaker:** He holds numerous batting records in both Test and One Day International (ODI) cricket, including the most runs and most centuries in both formats.
*   **Icon of Indian Cricket:** More than just a player, Tendulkar became a national idol and a symbol of hope and aspiration for millions in India, especially during his playing career.
*   **Longevity and Consistency:** His career spanned over two decades (1989-2013) at the highest level, during which he maintained exceptional standards of performance.
*   **Global Ambassador for Cricket:** His immense popularity transcended cricketing nations, making him one of the most recognized athletes worldwide.
*   **Role Model:** Known for his humble demeanor, sportsmanship, and dedication, he is widely respected for his conduct on and off the field.

### 2. Important Facts

*   **Full Name:** Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar
*   **Born:** April 24, 1973, Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
*   **Playing Role:** Right-handed top-order batsman
*   **International Debut:** November 15, 1989 (Test match vs. Pakistan) and December 18, 1989 (ODI vs. Pakistan).
*   **Career Span:** 1989 – 2013 (24 years)
*   **Key Records & Achievements:**
    *   **Most Runs in Test Cricket:** 15,921 runs
    *   **Most Runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs):** 18,426 runs
    *   **Most International Centuries:** 100 centuries (51 in Tests, 49 in ODIs) – a unique record.
    *   **Most Matches Played:** 200 Tests, 463 ODIs.
    *   **First Male Cricketer to Score an ODI Double Century:** 200* runs against South Africa on February 24, 2010.
    *   **2011 Cricket World Cup Winner:** A crucial member of the Indian team that won the ICC Cricket World Cup at home, fulfilling his long-standing dream.
    *   **Man of the Tournament:** ICC Cricket World Cup 2003.
*   **Awards and Honours:**
    *   **Bharat Ratna (2014):** India's highest civilian award, making him the youngest recipient and the first sportsperson to receive it.
    *   **Padma Vibhushan (2008):** India's second-highest civilian award.
    *   **Padma Shri (1999):** India's fourth-highest civilian award.
    *   **Arjuna Award (1994):** Award for outstanding performance in sports.
    *   **ICC Cricket Hall of Fame (2019):** Inducted for his illustrious career.
*   **Post-Retirement Activities:**
    *   Served as a Member of Parliament, Rajya Sabha (the upper house of India's Parliament) from 2012 to 2018.
    *   Continues to be involved in various charitable initiatives and sports promotion.
    *   UNICEF Ambassador.

### 3. Timeline

*   **1973:** Born in Mumbai, India.
*   **1989 (November):** Makes his international Test debut against Pakistan at just 16 years old.
*   **1990 (August):** Scores his first Test century against England at Old Trafford.
*   **1994 (September):** Scores his first ODI century against Australia in Sri Lanka.
*   **1999 (March):** Awarded the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award.
*   **2003:** Named Player of the Tournament at the ICC Cricket World Cup in South Africa, where India reached the final.
*   **2008 (January):** Becomes the leading run-scorer in Test cricket, surpassing Brian Lara's record.
*   **2008 (March):** Awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award.
*   **2010 (February):** Becomes the first male cricketer to score a double century in a One Day International match (200* vs. South Africa).
*   **2011 (April):** Realizes his dream of winning the ICC Cricket World Cup with India.
*   **2012 (March):** Scores his historic 100th international century against Bangladesh in an ODI.
*   **2012 (December):** Announces retirement from One Day International cricket.
*   **2013 (November):** Plays his 200th and final Test match against West Indies in Mumbai, retiring from all forms of international cricket.
*   **2014 (November):** Conferred with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
*   **2019 (July):** Inducted into the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame.
